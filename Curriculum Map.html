Course Overview

This AI Product Strategy course by Product School is a specialized certification designed to help product leaders build a comprehensive, "board-ready" AI strategy. The course is structured into six sequential modules, each focusing on a critical component of AI product development — from initial validation to final executive pitching.

The program moves beyond general product management to address the specific challenges of AI: data flywheels, cost structures, trust, and governance. By the end of the course, participants have assembled a Living Strategy Repo — a version-controlled GitHub repo with six interconnected components, ready for executive-level presentation.

Module Detail

Module 1 The Bet What should we build, and how do we know fast?
Focus: Identifying what to build and validating it quickly.
Learning Objectives

Diagnose where traditional strategy fails in AI, score product vulnerability, build a prototype, and lock in the first component of the strategy.

Lecture Topics
  • Why AI breaks traditional strategy: probabilistic outputs, variable infra costs, weekly capability shifts
  • AI-powered discovery: synthetic personas, automated insight synthesis, real-time segment simulation
  • The 5 AI Value Archetypes: Automator, Copilot, Oracle, Creator, Orchestrator
  • Probabilistic prioritization — confidence intervals, not commitments
  • The Three-Axis Vulnerability Diagnostic: Contextual Moat, Data Advantage, Platform Exposure
Hands-On Exercise

Score your product on the Three-Axis Vulnerability Diagnostic, partner-challenge your weakest axis (name a specific real attacker), then prototype your AI product idea in 15 minutes using v0, Cursor, or Lovable.

Output
  • Forked Living Strategy Repo (GitHub template)
  • 01-the-bet/diagnostic.md — vulnerability scores, rationale, named attacker, biggest vulnerability in one line
  • 01-the-bet/prototype.md — shareable link to a working clickable prototype + bet thesis + kill criteria
Module 2 The Moat Why won't this get copied in 6 months?
Focus: Defensive strategy and long-term sustainability.
Learning Objectives

Evaluate and score your data flywheel, stress-test the product against a 90-day competitor encroachment plan, and design a Kill Switch for risky vendor and model dependencies.

Lecture Topics
  • Data isn't a moat — loops are: System of Intelligence vs. System of Record
  • Zero-party data acquisition through correction and feedback UX
  • Cold-start survival: synthetic seeding, transfer learning, partnerships
  • Contextual moats via vertical depth and workflow embedding (switching costs)
  • Platform encroachment defense and the Model Dependency Kill Switch (abstraction layers, multi-model routing)
  • Competitive positioning on two strategic axes
Hands-On Exercise

Score each loop of your data flywheel and identify the weakest link. Place your product and 3–5 competitors on a competitive positioning map. Run a partner-led 90-day "Big Tech attacker" encroachment simulation against your weakest axis. Draft a portability checklist.

Output
  • 02-the-moat/data-flywheel.md — scored four-loop flywheel + Encroachment Threat Assessment + 90-Day Encroachment Plan
  • 02-the-moat/kill-switch.md — Vendor Dependency Assessment (Provider / Abstraction / Routing / Eval) + Portability Score + stress scenarios
Module 3 The Margin Will this make money or bleed it?
Focus: Monetization and unit economics of AI.
Learning Objectives

Map AI cost curves, design pricing models specific to AI outputs, and stress-test profit margins using cascading financial scenarios.

Lecture Topics
  • The margin war: 80% SaaS margins collapsing to 40–60% under AI inference cost
  • Model cascading: cheap triage model → frontier model for hard cases
  • CPO-level LLMOps levers: quantization, caching, routing, observability, GPU forecasting
  • Pricing evolution: hybrid (base + usage / outcome / credit), outcome-based, dynamic for agents and power users
  • Old SaaS revenue ($50/seat) vs. AI usage / outcome revenue ($2/successful automation)
  • Klarna case study: the Automator that overcorrected
Hands-On Exercise

Build a cost curve with real inference pricing, design a hybrid pricing strategy matched to your value archetype, and stress-test with the Scenario Lab — drag sliders for inference cost multiplier (1x → 3x), volume growth, and cascading ratio to watch margin behavior in real time. Partner stress-tests the model.

Output
  • 03-the-margin/cost-curve.md — Cost Model + Cascading Strategy + Pricing Model + Stress Tests + Board One-Pager
Module 4 The Contract Why will users trust a probabilistic system?
Focus: Trust, reliability, and user experience.
Learning Objectives

Build "golden datasets," design UX that communicates AI confidence levels to users, and draft a reliability contract that sets explicit performance expectations.

Lecture Topics
  • Golden datasets and ground truth as product infrastructure
  • Human-in-the-loop architecture for 99.9% reliability in regulated verticals
  • LLM-as-a-Judge for regression testing, scaling evals, and drift detection
  • Confidence scores and probabilistic UX primitives — showing uncertainty, not hiding it
  • Trust ≠ accuracy: perceived control beats raw percentage points
  • Air Canada chatbot case: when an AI product made promises the company had to honor
Hands-On Exercise

Build a Golden Dataset Spec with adversarial rows. Design a tiered Confidence UX with three explicit thresholds (Confident >90%, Uncertain 50-90%, Not confident <50%). Red-team your own system with a partner to surface silent-failure modes. Draft a Reliability Contract with measurable targets and alert thresholds.

Output
  • 04-the-contract/golden-dataset.md — Golden Dataset Spec + Confidence UX Design + Reliability Contract + HITL Architecture + Red-Team Findings
Module 5 The Guardrails What breaks when this scales?
Focus: Scaling safely and governance.
Learning Objectives

Design systems that compound value as they scale, draft internal AI governance policies, and conduct a "shadow AI audit" to surface hidden risks.

Lecture Topics
  • Compounding systems vs. scaling systems: recursive learning, feedback loops, knowledge that compounds across teams
  • Connecting context: breaking domain silos so the system learns from every interaction
  • Responsible AI as competitive differentiator (EU AI Act, explainability, board-level risk translation)
  • Multi-agent orchestration governance: autonomy boundaries, tool-calling safety, memory management
  • ROI evaluation for agentic systems vs. traditional automation
  • Shadow AI audit: surfacing unsanctioned tools (Spotify: 1,000 tools → 15 after audit)
  • Samsung data leak case study
Hands-On Exercise

Sketch a compounding system architecture for your product (where does quality improve with usage?). Draft a governance policy with agent-autonomy boundaries. Run a shadow AI audit of your own org — list unsanctioned tools, owners, risk level. Partner audits feedback loops and agent boundaries.

Output
  • 05-the-guardrails/compounding-system.md — Feedback Loops + Context Connectivity + Governance Policy + Agent Topology + Shadow AI Audit
Module 6 The Pitch How do you get this funded, shipped, and adopted?
Focus: Communication and leadership buy-in.
Learning Objectives

Assemble all components into a final strategy, build an AI-powered product roadmap, and pitch the strategy to a mock "Board of Directors" for funding and approval.

Lecture Topics
  • Multi-horizon roadmaps in the continuous-sprint era: H1 quick wins + H2 bets + H3 moonshots
  • AI-specific board metrics: hallucination rate, drift velocity, trust scores, inference ROI
  • Pilot-to-production change management and adoption barriers
  • Board-ready storytelling: translating AI system complexity into investment narratives
  • AI GTM frictions: outcome packaging, trust barriers, demo strategy for probabilistic products
  • Duolingo case study: the AI transformation narrative to investors
Hands-On Exercise

Assemble all five prior components into a cohesive README.md board narrative. Build a three-horizon roadmap with kill criteria for H2 bets and at least one H3 experiment. Run your strategy through an AI self-evaluation prompt across all six rubric dimensions. Pitch to the room (5 min present + 3 min Q&A) — the class acts as your board.

Output
  • 06-the-pitch/roadmap.md — Three-Horizon Roadmap (H1 Ship 0-4 weeks / H2 Validate 1-3 months / H3 Explore 3-6 months) + AI Evaluation (6 lenses) + Board Pitch + M1 Baseline vs. Now
  • README.md — board-ready narrative summarizing the complete strategy (a colleague should understand it cold without opening sub-folders)
  • Final deliverable: the complete Living Strategy Repo URL — six folders, version-controlled, shareable Monday morning

Final Project — The Living Strategy Repo

Participants don't build a final project at the end — they build it every session. The submission is a single GitHub repo URL containing all six components, evaluated across six dimensions: Bet Clarity, Defensibility, Economics, Trust Architecture, Governance, and Narrative & Roadmap.

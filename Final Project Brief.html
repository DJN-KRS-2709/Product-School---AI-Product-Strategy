Final Project

Your Living Strategy

Build an AI product strategy across 6 sessions. Present it to the room. Take it back to work.

What you submit: A GitHub repo link containing your complete AI product strategy.

When: Module 6 — you present directly from your repo to the class (your "board").

How it's built: One component per module. By M6, you assemble and pitch.

What You're Building

Your final project is a Living Strategy Repo — a version-controlled GitHub repository that contains six strategy components, one per module. This isn't a slide deck or a doc. It's a working, shareable, evolving strategy artifact you can use at work after the course.

1
The Bet (Module 1)
Diagnose your product's vulnerability across three axes (moat, data, platform risk). Write a killer memo from a competitor's perspective. Build a working prototype.
Artifacts: diagnostic.md + prototype.md
2
The Moat (Module 2)
Score your data flywheel, map competitive positioning, run a 90-day encroachment plan against yourself, and build a vendor Kill Switch with portability actions.
Artifacts: data-flywheel.md + kill-switch.md
3
The Margin (Module 3)
Map your cost curve with real inference pricing, design a pricing strategy (access vs. outcome vs. hybrid), and build a board-ready one-pager showing before/after AI economics.
Artifacts: cost-curve.md + board one-pager
4
The Contract (Module 4)
Build a golden dataset specification, design confidence UX tiers, red-team your own system, and write a reliability contract with explicit promises and escalation paths.
Artifacts: golden-dataset.md + reliability contract
5
The Guardrails (Module 5)
Design a compounding system (not just a scaling one), draft a governance policy, run a shadow AI audit of your org, and define agent autonomy boundaries.
Artifacts: compounding-system.md + governance policy + audit
6
The Pitch (Module 6)
Assemble all five components into a cohesive narrative. Build a three-horizon roadmap using AI. Evaluate your strategy with an AI prompt. Present to the room as your board.
Artifacts: roadmap.md + README.md (your board narrative)

The Pitch (Module 6)

In the final session, you present your strategy to the class. The class acts as your board of directors. No slides — you present directly from your repo.

Format

5 minutes to present your strategy: the bet, the moat, the margin, the trust architecture, the governance approach, and the roadmap. 3 minutes of Q&A from the room — your classmates ask the hardest questions they can think of.

What Good Looks Like

A README.md that a colleague could read cold and understand the complete strategy without opening any sub-folders. A narrative that leads with the problem and the user — not the technology. A roadmap with clear horizons, kill criteria for H2 bets, and at least one H3 experiment.

What You Submit

Submission

Your GitHub repo link

One URL. Everything inside it. Version-controlled, portable, yours to keep.

Evaluation Criteria

Your strategy is evaluated across six dimensions — the same lenses you learned in Modules 1 through 5, plus the overall narrative.

Bet Clarity
Is the product bet specific? Evidence-backed thesis, named target user, clear "why now." Not vague aspirations.
Defensibility
Honest moat assessment with compounding loops — not just "we have data." Kill switch shows vendor optionality.
Economics
Cost model with real numbers. Pricing strategy that matches the value archetype. Margin story that holds under stress.
Trust Architecture
Golden dataset spec with adversarial cases. Confidence UX tiers. Reliability contract with explicit promises.
Governance
Compounding system (not just scaling). Agent boundaries defined. Shadow AI acknowledged and triaged.
Narrative & Roadmap
README tells a coherent story. Three-horizon roadmap with kill criteria. M1 vs. M6 baseline shows growth.

FAQ

Do I need to know how to code?
No. The repo uses markdown files (plain text with simple formatting). GitHub's web editor works fine — no terminal or coding required. The prototype in M1 uses AI tools that generate code for you.
What if I miss a session?
Each module's template has clear prompts and instructions. You can complete missed components on your own using the interactive tools and the slides. The repo template guides you through every exercise.
Do I work on a real product or a hypothetical?
A real product at your company. The exercises are designed to produce artifacts you can actually use at work. Hypotheticals produce weaker strategies and less useful feedback from partners.
Can I keep the repo after the course?
Yes — it's yours. The repo lives in your GitHub account permanently. You can continue updating it, share it with colleagues, or use it as a template for future strategy work.
What do I need before the first class?
Free accounts on: (1) GitHub, (2) one AI prototyping tool — we recommend v0.dev, and (3) one AI chat tool like ChatGPT or Claude. That's it. Everything else runs in your browser.

## Slide 1 — Title

- 6-session AI Product Strategy certification for senior PMs who already know AI basics (AIPC or equivalent). They need the strategic playbook: what to build, how to defend it, how to price it, how to govern it, how to pitch it.
- The big idea: participants build one living strategy across all 6 sessions, on their real product at their company. Not theory. Not a document they review once a year. A working strategy they can present Monday morning.

## Slide 4 — The Arc

- Backbone of the entire course. Six strategic questions, asked in order, each depending on the last:
  - **The Bet** — What should we build, and how do we know fast?
  - **The Moat** — Why won't this get copied in 6 months?
  - **The Margin** — Will this make money or bleed it?
  - **The Contract** — Why will users trust a probabilistic system?
  - **The Guardrails** — What breaks when this scales?
  - **The Pitch** — How do you get this funded, shipped, and adopted?
- You can't price something you can't defend. You can't govern something users don't trust. Each module builds on the last — that's why it's one program, not six.
- **Most important slide. Slow down. Point to each node.**

---

## Slide 5 — The Living Strategy

- The single biggest design decision. Not a document. Not a PRD. Not a dissertation. A **living strategy** — interactive, compounding, co-creating with you. Carlos's words: "Something that has a life of its own, beating and iterating at a much higher frequency."
- Five interconnected components built across M1–M5. M6 integrates and presents.
- Components, not layers. Not sequential steps — interconnected pillars. Build order is sequential (you can't price what you can't defend), but iteration order differs in practice.
- Each session's build connects to the final artifact. By M6 they don't start from scratch — they assemble 5 components they've already built.
- **"They don't leave with a certificate. They leave with a weapon."**

## Slide 7 — Wow Moments

- Carlos cares about this. Every module has one peak moment that makes participants lean forward. And crucially: each wow moment produces something **tangible** they can take home. Progress they can feel.
- **M1: The Prototype Bet** — build a working AI product in 15 min with v0/Cursor/Lovable
- **M2: The Kill Switch** — design vendor portability to swap AI models in 48hrs. "OpenAI just 3x'd your cost. Can you survive?"
- **M3: The Margin Shock** — model your real product's cost curve. Watch 80% SaaS margins collapse to 40% on your own numbers. Nobody else teaches this.
- **M4: The Trust Inversion** — side-by-side demo: 85% accuracy with audit trail beats 95% without. Counter-intuitive and sticky.
- **M5: The Shadow AI Audit** — 5-minute checklist reveals unsanctioned AI tools and hidden spend. (Spotify example: 1,000 tools → 15 after audit.)
- **M6: The Board Simulation** — present the full strategy. Room plays the board. Re-read M1 baseline to see distance traveled.
- **"Six modules. Six wow moments. Zero filler."**

## Slide 8 — M1: The Bet

- M1's job: break the old mental model and replace it with probabilistic thinking.
- Opens with 3 True/False provocations — all FALSE. "The best AI product wins?" "You can predict which feature wins?" "Your discovery process is fast enough?" By #3, the room knows this isn't a beginner course.
- Live diagnosis on a product everyone knows (Notion, Figma, Slack) using the Three-Axis Diagnostic: contextual moat, data advantage, platform exposure. Then they apply it to their own product at their company.
- Peer challenge: partner must lower at least one score. Then name one real AI-native competitor attacking that vulnerability. The outside-in pressure reveals blind spots self-assessment always misses.
- **Wow moment: build a working prototype with AI tools in 15 minutes.** Not slides. The thing itself.
- They leave with: Strategy component 1 (The Bet) + a shareable prototype link. Both carry forward to M2.

---

## Slide 9 — M2 & M3

- **M2 — The Moat:** Data alone isn't a moat — workflow embedding and switching costs are. Map the data flywheel, score it, find the weakest link. Partner plays Big Tech attacker. Wow moment: The Kill Switch — design vendor portability to swap models in 48 hours. Most will realize they can't.
- **M3 — The Margin:** The module nobody else teaches at depth. 2 full hours on AI economics. Model cascading (cheap model for 80%, frontier for 20%), CPO-level LLMOps levers, hybrid pricing evolution. Build a real cost curve and stress-test it. Wow moment: watching 80% SaaS margins collapse to 40% on their own numbers. They feel it in their gut.
- Case studies: Jasper vs. ChatGPT (the moat that wasn't), Klarna (the Automator that overcorrected).
- **M3 is our highest-differentiation module. The one people will talk about.**

---

## Slide 10 — M4 & M5

- **M4 — The Contract:** Trust ≠ accuracy. It's about perceived control. Golden datasets, LLM-as-a-Judge, HITL as product feature, confidence UX. Wow moment: side-by-side proving 85% with transparency beats 95% without. Connects to our existing Evals course but stays strategic.
- **M5 — The Guardrails:** Two things. First: building compounding systems — infrastructure that gets better with every iteration, feedback loops that connect context across teams so knowledge compounds instead of siloing. Second: governance that closes enterprise deals. Shadow AI audit wow moment: 5-minute checklist reveals hidden spend and tools nobody knew about.
- Carlos said this module needs to be "meaty" — not data policies, but building systems that learn and compound. We're designing it around that.
- Strategic 20%, not operational 80%. Connects to standalone Evals and Agents courses without replacing them.

## Slide 11 — M6: The Capstone

- Everything comes together. The living strategy they've been building for 5 modules gets integrated, refined, stress-tested.
- Board-ready storytelling: translating AI complexity into investment narratives. Multi-horizon roadmaps. AI GTM frictions.
- Capstone: 60–75 min. Present the complete living strategy. Room plays the board. "Why should we fund this?"
- Emotional kicker: re-read M1 baseline, compare to the full strategy. Distance traveled is the proof.
- **Intense but supportive. Hostile but friendly.**

## Slide 12 — Design Principles

- 5 design choices. The two that matter most:
  - **Build every session** — every module has a hands-on build moment that produces something tangible. Not just a feeling — something they take home.
  - **Opinionated throughout** — bold provocations in every module. Opinions attract seniors.
- Also: consistent case study format (Bet → Crack → Correction), escalating peer challenge, external-first language.

## Instructions for Gamma

## Slide 11 — Design Principles

**Five Things That Make This Best-in-Class**

Numbered list with brief descriptions:

1. **Build in every session** — Prototype in M1. Cost model in M3. Eval dashboard in M4. Governance policy in M5. Pitch in M6.
2. **Opinionated throughout** — "Your data is not your moat." "AI will compress your margins." Every module opens with a provocation.
3. **One case study format, repeated** — Bet → Crack → Correction. Klarna, Jasper, Air Canada, Samsung, Duolingo.
4. **Peer challenge is structural** — Partner attacks your moat, stress-tests your pricing. M6 is a board simulation.
5. **External-first language** — Every question is about the product, not the PM.

## Design Notes for Gamma

- **Theme:** Dark background (#0f0f13 or similar), warm gold accent (#D4A73A), white headlines, muted grey body text
- **Font:** Inter or similar clean sans-serif
- **Key visuals:** The arc flow (slide 4), the artifact layers (slide 5), and the 3-lane positioning (slide 3) are the most important visual moments
- **Slide 1:** Large title, centered, minimal. Let the words do the work.
- **Slide 3:** Product School card should be visually elevated/highlighted vs. the greyed-out competitors
- **Slide 4:** Horizontal flow diagram is the centerpiece — give it space
- **Slide 5:** Stacked layers should feel like they're building on each other, top to bottom
- **Slide 8–9:** Side-by-side module cards — use each module's accent color on the border
- **Slide 12:** 2x2 grid; card 1 (Strategy Brief) should be visually dominant (gold border or larger)
- **Slide 16:** Closing should feel clean and confident. One statement, one visual arc. End on the question.
- Keep text minimal — this deck is designed to be spoken, not read

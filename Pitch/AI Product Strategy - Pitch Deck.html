Design Principles

Five Things That Make
This Best-in-Class

1
Build in every session
Prototype in M1. Cost model in M3. Eval dashboard in M4. Governance policy in M5. Pitch in M6. Every session has a hands-on build moment.
2
Opinionated throughout
"Your data is not your moat." "AI will compress your margins before it expands them." Every module opens with a provocation. Opinions attract seniors.
3
One case study format, repeated
Bet → Crack → Correction. Klarna, Jasper, Air Canada, Samsung, Duolingo. Same 3-act structure every module.
4
Peer challenge is structural
Partner attacks your moat, stress-tests your pricing, red-teams your evals. Stakes escalate. M6 is a full board simulation.
5
External-first language
Every question is about the product, not the PM. "Build a cost curve you can defend to your board" — not "reframe your thinking."

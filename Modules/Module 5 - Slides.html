Case Study

Samsung: Shadow AI Meets Real Data

The Bet
Samsung · ChatGPT for code, notes, meetings—fast wins, no guardrails.
The Crack
3 leaks / ~1 month · source · meetings · fab data → vendor pipeline · no policy / no trail
The Correction
Ban + rebuild internal AI · Lesson: absence of governance = strategy failure

Samsung is the version of shadow AI your CISO manages. The version you can act on is what your users are quietly building around your product — that's the next slide.

Framework

The Shadow AI Audit (for PMs)

What are your users building with AI around your product? Discover → Signal → Prioritize → Decide.
The reframe → Samsung-style shadow AI is your CISO's problem (employee tool sprawl). The version you can act on is what your users are quietly building with AI on top of, alongside, or to replace pieces of your product. That's where your next roadmap lives.
1
Discover
Support tickets · user interviews · Zapier/Make recipes · forum posts · API patterns (large exports = enrichment elsewhere)
2
Signal
Why are they reaching out? Workflow gap · trust gap · capability gap · pricing gap
3
Prioritize
Frequency × strategic relevance. Which workarounds repeat? Which would unlock real value if native?
4
Decide
Build (absorb the capability) · Partner (official integration) · Ignore (not strategic — let users keep using the external tool)

Best discovery research nobody runs — your users are already telling you what's missing. They just don't open a ticket called "feature request."

💡 example
Scope
What this policy covers: _____
AI features in the SupportCopilot product line — automated reply drafting, routing, and resolution scoring. Excludes internal-only analytics dashboards (covered by separate data-team policy).
💡 example
Autonomy boundaries
OK solo: _____ · Needs human: _____
Drafting replies — auto. Sending replies under $0 risk (FAQ, account info) — auto. Sending replies with promised remedies (refunds, credits, escalations) — human approval required, even if AI confidence > 95%. Closing tickets without reply — never auto.
💡 example
Escalation triggers
When humans must enter: _____
(1) Confidence < 70%. (2) Customer message flagged legal, medical, or distressed. (3) Any reply mentioning refund, credit, or policy exception. (4) Customer requests human. (5) ≥3 turns in one conversation.
💡 example
Audit cadence
How often we review: real-time / daily / weekly / monthly / quarterly
Weekly — automated eval vs. golden dataset (PM: Sam). Monthly — human review of 50 random conversations + all escalations (Legal: Priya). Quarterly — full policy review with security + legal (CTO sign-off).
💡 example
Regulatory exposure (EU AI Act / GDPR / sector)
Regimes that apply: _____ · Risk tier: minimal / limited / high
EU AI Act — limited risk (customer service is non-prohibited). GDPR — applies; data minimization in prompts, no training on customer PII. SOC 2 controls in place for log retention.
Agent Topology (if you ship agents): what each agent can do · what it can't · who approves

Key Takeaways

The Guardrails

Compounding beats scaling. Most AI products scale without getting smarter. If your system doesn't compound — corrections feeding training, cross-domain transfer, network intelligence — it's a commodity with variable costs.

Governance done well is a GTM asset. Most orgs plateau at compliance (Level 1). Enterprise buyers increasingly ask for governance artifacts. The jump from compliance to sellable trust accelerates deals.

Shadow AI is a strategy failure, not a compliance gap. Samsung's lesson: three data leaks in a month, no policy, no trail. The absence of governance isn't neutral — it's actively degrading your AI strategy.

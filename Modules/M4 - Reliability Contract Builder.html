Accuracy
How often the AI gets the answer right
💡 Tip. The point isn't the highest number — it's a defensible number you can hit AND measure weekly. 99% sounds great but you can't ship it.
🟢 90–95% defensible 🟡 99%+ vanity 🔴 <85% rebuild
🚨
Hallucination rate
How often the AI confidently invents facts
💡 Tip. Auto-rollback, not page. Air Canada is why — the bot speaks for the brand, and the brand pays for the lie.
🟢 <1% defensible 🟡 <2% acceptable 🔴 ≥2% Air-Canada zone
⚡
Latency (p95)
How long the slowest 5% of users wait
💡 Tip. The only metric measured continuously. Naming the tooling here (Datadog, PagerDuty) signals SRE rigor to your CTO.
🟢 <500ms (chat) 🟢 <2s (agent) 🔴 >5s losing users
🔄
Drift velocity
How fast quality decays over time
💡 Tip. Triggers a gold-set audit, not a model rollback. The world changed, not the model. If you rollback, you ship yesterday's truth.
🟢 <0.5%/4w defensible 🟡 <1%/4w watch 🔴 >1%/4w investigate
🤝
HITL Architecture
When does a human enter? Who reviews? Does it feed back?
💡 Tip. Without "feeds back into the gold set," HITL is a crutch. With it, every correction becomes a training signal. That's the M2 flywheel connection.

Provocation · 5 Minutes

The Roadmap Is an Output,
Not Your Job Description.

Vote first. Then we reveal. Then we argue.
FALSE
"Our roadmap IS our strategy."
A roadmap is the output of strategic decisions, not the strategy itself. Leading with a feature list means you've confused the plan with the thinking.
FALSE
"AI metrics are the same as product metrics."
AI products need: hallucination rate, drift velocity, confidence distribution, HITL rate, inference ROI, eval regression frequency. Your board needs these alongside MAU and retention.
FALSE
"If the product is good, it sells itself."
AI faces unique GTM frictions: probabilistic demos, error asymmetry, governance gates. Good product is necessary. Not sufficient for adoption.

Lecture · Point 2 of 4

AI Metrics Boards Care About

Beyond MAU and retention — six metrics your board needs to see.
Hallucination Rate
% outputs with fabricated info. Board: liability.
Drift Velocity
Quality degradation speed. Board: silent failure.
Confidence Distribution
High vs low confidence %. Board: quality.
HITL Rate
% needing human intervention. Board: scalability.
Inference ROI
Revenue per $ of inference. Board: margin.
Eval Regression
How often updates break quality. Board: reliability.

Gokul: Revenue durability > growth rate. Retention is the fundamental indicator of quality.

Lecture · Point 3 of 4

Translating AI Into Investment Narratives

The board doesn't want a tech briefing. They want four answers.
1
"Why should we invest?" → The Bet. Market, competition, opportunity.
2
"Why won't someone copy this?" → The Moat. Flywheel, workflow depth, moat scorecard.
3
"What's the financial model?" → The Margin. Cost curve, pricing, P&L, stress tests.
4
"What could go wrong?" → Contract + Guardrails. Trust, eval, governance.

Anti-pattern: leading with technology. Lead with the investment thesis.

Lecture · Point 4 of 4

Selling Probabilistic Products

Four frictions unique to AI go-to-market.
The Demo Problem
AI outputs are probabilistic. Can't guarantee demo results. Use curated scenarios, confidence-filtered outputs.
Error Asymmetry
Users fear AI errors > human errors. A mistake means "tech doesn't work." Frame errors as learning, show improvement.
The Governance Gate
Enterprise: CISO review before product demo. No governance docs → no meeting. M5 framework = sales collateral.
The Adoption Curve
Trust before adopt. First experience must be high-confidence, high-value, low-risk. Progressive autonomy.
Case Study · 7 Minutes

The AI Transformation Narrative

The Bet
Duolingo integrated GPT-4 — AI roleplay conversations, detailed explanations. 500M+ users. Thesis: AI transforms language learning from repetition into conversation.
The Crack
Engagement variance. "AI is teaching me" competed with gamified identity. Investor questions shifted: "Does AI actually improve learning?"
The Correction
Reframed: AI enhances the loop, doesn't replace it. Published outcome data. Led investor decks with retention + learning outcomes, not technology.

Is your AI narrative built on technology or outcomes?

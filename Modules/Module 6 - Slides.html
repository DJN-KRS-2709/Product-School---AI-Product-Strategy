Provocation

Roadmap = Output,
Not Strategy.

Poll each claim — then unpack.

FALSE
"Our roadmap IS our strategy."
Roadmap = decisions made visible. Features without thesis hide the thinking.
FALSE
"AI metrics = product metrics."
Add quality + cost signals (hallucinations, drift, HITL, eval regressions) with MAU/retention.
FALSE
"Great product sells itself."
AI adds messy demos, scarier errors, security reviews — necessary ≠ sufficient.

Lecture · Point 2 of 4

AI Metrics Boards Ask For

Six signals beyond MAU / retention

Hallucination Rate
% outputs with fabricated info. Board: liability.
Drift Velocity
Quality degradation speed. Board: silent failure.
Confidence Distribution
High vs low confidence %. Board: quality.
HITL Rate
% needing human intervention. Board: scalability.
Inference ROI
Revenue per $ of inference. Board: margin.
Eval Regression
How often updates break quality. Board: reliability.

Durability beats hype · retention = quality

Key Takeaways

The Pitch

A roadmap is not a strategy. Strategy answers why this bet, why now, and why us. The roadmap follows after the story is clear. Lead with the thesis, not the backlog.

The living strategy is the artifact. Not a 20-page document. Not a slide deck. A visual, interactive dashboard that your team can open, update, and present. It compounds — just like your product should.

Say it out loud. The board simulation proved it: strategies that survive scrutiny are the ones that have been rehearsed, stress-tested, and refined by people who disagree with you.

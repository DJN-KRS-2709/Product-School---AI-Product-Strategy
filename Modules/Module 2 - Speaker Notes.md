# Module 2 — The Moat

## Slide 1 — Title
- Today we find out if anyone can copy what they're building in 6 months
- They'll walk out with a scored flywheel, a vendor portability plan, and Component 2

## Slide 2 — Agenda
- Starts with a hands-on activity — they're working before we even lecture
- Peer challenge is renamed: "The 90-Day Encroachment Plan" — partner builds a concrete week-by-week plan to steal their users
- Kill Switch at the end is the wow moment
- There's 2 min of housekeeping buffer at the top for cameras, tools, ground rules

## Slide 3 — Moat Stress Test (Opening Activity)
- They pull out their M1 Three-Axis Scorecard
- Find their lowest score — that's their crack
- Then name one specific company that could exploit that weakness. Not "Big Tech" — a real name
- 2 min to write, 3 min to share. Sets up the entire lecture

## Slide 4 — The 8 Moats Landscape
- Gokul's framework: Data, Workflow, Regulatory, Distribution, Ecosystem, Network, Physical, Scale
- 4+ total = secure, 2-3 = weak, 0-1 = in trouble
- Quick example: Atlassian ~3 (data, workflow, ecosystem), Monday ~1 (light workflow). Both down 75%
- For AI builders, Data and Workflow are the two that matter
- Mention the System of Record vs. System of Intelligence distinction here: Record stores data, Intelligence learns from it. Moat = the delta between what a foundation model knows and what your system has learned

## Slide 5 — The Data Flywheel
- Three types of data that compound: corrections, preferences, domain context
- The cycle: interact → capture → improve → better outputs → more interaction
- Key question: does your product capture this stuff or throw it away?

## Slide 6 — Contextual Moats + Cold-Start Sidebar
- Horizontal AI = bolted on, easy to copy. Vertical AI = embedded so deep that leaving means rebuilding the process
- GitHub Copilot — not just autocomplete, it's in the editor, PRs, review, CI. Leaving means changing how the team writes software
- Workflow depth is a spectrum: NetSuite (deep) vs. Zendesk (light)
- Cold-start sidebar for early-stage folks: synthetic seeding, HITL bootstrap, partnership data. It's temporary — the question is whether you're designed to escape it

## Slide 7 — Case Study: Jasper vs. ChatGPT
- Bet: $125M raised, $1.5B valuation, 70K customers, fastest AI startup 2022
- Crack: ChatGPT launched, OpenAI added plugins, Google shipped Gemini into Workspace. AI writing became free and native
- Correction: pivoted to enterprise marketing workflows. Gokul said "from 1 to 100 and 100 to 40." Bolt-on AI doesn't change your defensibility score
- Ask the room: where is your product a wrapper vs. a workflow?

## Slide 8 — Data Flywheel Scoring
- Four loops: Correction, Preference, Domain Context, Network
- Score each 1-5. Weakest loop = where competitors attack first

## Slide 9 — Break

## Slide 10 — Map Your Data Flywheel
- 15 min individual — score four loops, write what they actually capture today
- "We plan to build this" is a 1, not a 3

## Slide 11 — Encroachment Threat Assessment
- Three vectors: platform encroachment (Big Tech), vertical competitor (deeper in niche), adjacent expansion (company next door adds your feature)
- Name the attacker, the vector, time-to-threat, % of value at risk

## Slide 12 — The 90-Day Encroachment Plan (Peer Challenge)
- 6 min per person
- Defender presents flywheel + threats
- Encroacher builds a concrete 90-day plan: weeks 1-4 what they ship, weeks 5-8 how they poach users, weeks 9-12 why users don't come back
- Target the weakest flywheel loop — that's the entry point
- If they're being too polite: "the market won't be nice"

## Slide 13 — The Kill Switch
- Three layers: abstraction layer (no direct provider calls), multi-model routing (tasks go to different models by cost/quality), eval harness (automated quality check before switching)
- Gokul's point: switching costs heading to zero, agents can move data across tools, UX can be cloned
- If you can't swap in 48 hours, you have a dependency, not a strategy

## Slide 14 — Your Portability Audit
- 15 min build moment — audit vendor dependency
- Two stress tests: provider doubles pricing, provider restricts your use case
- Walk out with three action items: this week, this month, this quarter

# Module 1 — The Bet

## Slide 1 — Title
- We're diagnosing where their product strategy breaks under AI
- They build their first living strategy piece today

## Slide 2 — Agenda
- 2 hours, mostly applied work
- Three exercises, a case study, a prototype build

## Slide 3 — Opening Question
- Quick show of hands — adding AI to existing product or building AI-native?
- Ground rule: pick a real product at their company, not a startup idea

## Slide 4 — Baseline Write
- 3 minutes, silent, individual
- "Your CEO asks what's our AI strategy" — they write 3 sentences
- They'll compare this to their M6 answer at the end of the course

## Slide 5 — True/False Provocation
- Three statements, all FALSE — they vote first, then we reveal
- "Best AI product wins" — nope, copycats everywhere, model providers ship your feature for free. You need moats, that's M2
- "You can predict which AI feature wins" — nope, biggest wins come from non-consumption markets, behaviors that didn't exist before
- "Your discovery process is fast enough" — nope, traditional is 6-8 weeks, AI-powered is days

## Slide 6 — The Arc
- Six modules, six strategic questions, each builds on the last
- They're building one living strategy across M1-M5, then presenting it in M6
- Not worksheets — a real working strategy, board-ready by the end

## Slide 7 — Annual Cycles Are Dead
- The old model assumed you could commit to outcomes — AI broke that
- New model: "we have 70% confidence this delivers Y, here's our kill criteria"

## Slide 8 — Discovery Is Too Slow
- Notion went from a 6-week research cycle to 4 days using AI
- Synthetic personas, automated synthesis, segment simulation
- Ask the room: how long does your discovery take?

## Slide 9 — Probabilistic Thinking
- Old way: "we'll ship X by Q3" — new way: "70% confidence, here's the validation plan"
- Bard → Gemini wasn't a marketing fail, it was a narrative pivot
- Big insight: Shopify, Gamma, Granola — they created behaviors that didn't exist. TAM looks small because the behavior is new
- Fun aside if there's time: Gokul passed on Quinc at $100M saying "D2C is dead" — it raised at $10B

## Slide 10 — Case Study: Notion
- Bet: AI could turn their editor into a thinking tool
- Crack: traditional research would've taken 6-8 weeks, competitors ship first
- Correction: AI-assisted clustering → 4-day cycle, shipped while others researched

## Slide 11 — 5 AI Value Archetypes
- Not all AI bets are the same — know which one you're building
- Automator (Klarna) — replaces labor, margin pressure
- Copilot (GitHub) — augments humans, human stays in loop
- Oracle (Palantir) — predicts stuff, high willingness to pay
- Creator (Jasper) — generates content, getting copied fast
- Orchestrator — multi-agent, highest complexity
- They need to know which one is their product

## Slide 12 — Break
- 5 min

## Slide 13 — Three-Axis Diagnostic
- Three axes: Contextual Moat, Data Advantage, Platform Exposure
- Score each 1-5 — this carries into M2

## Slide 14 — Live Diagnosis
- 15 min group exercise — pick a known product (Notion, Figma, Linear, Slack) and diagnose it together
- Let the room argue, that's the point

## Slide 15 — Your Diagnostic
- 10 min individual — score their own product
- Tell them to be ruthless, their M2 partner will attack it

## Slide 16 — Pair Challenge
- 5 min per person
- Partner has to find at least one axis that should be lower
- Has to name a real AI-native competitor going after that weakness

## Slide 17 — In the Mind of a Killer
- Silent writing — pretend you're the Head of AI at the platform that wants to destroy your product
- 3-sentence memo: what you attack, your wedge, why users switch
- A few volunteers share

## Slide 18 — The Prototype Bet
- 15 min — take one idea and make it real with v0, Cursor, or Lovable
- Minimum: a shareable link
- This is where you see who builds vs. who just talks

## Slide 19 — Synthesis
- They fill in Component 1 of the living strategy
- What they're building, for whom, why now, archetype, scores, biggest vulnerability

## Slide 20 — Bridge to M2
- M2 is about whether anyone can copy this in 6 months
- Bring: scorecard, Component 1, prototype

## Slide 21 — Survey
- QR code / link

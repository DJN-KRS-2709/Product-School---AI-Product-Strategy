Scope
What this policy covers — and what it does not
💡 Tip. Be specific about what's IN. Use the exclusion line so reviewers don't wonder "what about X?"

Autonomy boundaries
For each decision: who gets to make it?
💡 Tip. Three levels: 🟢 auto, 🟡 human approval, 🔴 never auto. The hardest line is human-approval even when AI confidence is high — that's how you avoid Air Canada.
🚦
Escalation triggers
Conditions that route to a human, every time
💡 Tip. Triggers must be measurable. "Customer is unhappy" isn't a trigger. "Customer message contains refund / legal / distressed language" is.
🔁
Audit cadence
Stack cadences — each row gets a named owner
💡 Tip. Continuous catches drift, periodic catches systemic issues. No row without an owner — "the team" is not an owner.
⚖️
Regulatory exposure
EU AI Act · GDPR · sector regimes
💡 Tip. Name the regimes, then name the controls. Reviewers care more about controls than the risk tier alone.

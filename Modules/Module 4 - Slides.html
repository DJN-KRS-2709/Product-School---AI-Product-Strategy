Provocation · 8 Minutes

95% Accuracy With No Audit Trail
Loses to 85% With One.

Vote first. Then we reveal. Then we argue.
FALSE
"Higher accuracy = more trust."
Trust is a function of perceived control, not accuracy %. An 85% system with confidence scores, explanations, and user override is trusted more than a 95% black box.
FALSE
"Users don't care how the AI works."
They don't care about transformers. They care about reliability signals — confidence scores, source citations, "I'm not sure" messages, and the ability to correct. Legibility is a product feature.
FALSE
"We'll add evals later, after we ship."
AI degrades silently — model drift, data shift, prompt injection. By the time users complain, trust is gone. Evals are launch infrastructure, not technical debt.
Lecture · Point 1 of 4

Trust ≠ Accuracy

Control and legibility beat a vanity accuracy number.
Black Box
95%
· No scores · No override
· Surprises in production
Legible System
85%
· Scores + sources · “Not sure”
· User can correct

Real world: Tesla/ADAS-style UIs force handoffs · Perplexity / AI Overviews lead with sources

Understandable + controllable wins — even at a lower benchmark %.

HITL as a shrinking queue — not permanent babysitting.

Key distinction: A System of Record stores data. A System of Intelligence learns from it. Your moat lives in the delta between what a foundation model knows out of the box and what your system has learned. If the delta is zero — no moat.

Lecture · The Engine

The Data Flywheel

Zero-party data and compounding loops — the engine of AI defensibility.
Users interact
↓
System captures signal
↓
Model improves
↓ ↻
Better outputs → more usage
Three types of data that compound:
1. Corrections
When users fix an AI output, that correction is proprietary training signal no one else has.
2. Preferences
Every thumbs-up/down, every edit, every "show me something different" builds a taste profile unique to your users.
3. Domain context
Accumulated knowledge of how this workflow works in this industry. Generic models start from zero here.

Does your product capture these — or throw them away?

Lecture · Depth + Cold-Start

Vertical Depth Beats Horizontal Features

Switching costs come from workflow depth, not feature quality.
Horizontal AI Feature
Weak Moat
Bolted-on AI assistant. Easy for the platform to replicate. Users can switch in a weekend.
Vertical AI Workflow
Strong Moat
AI so embedded the product is the workflow. Ripping it out = rebuilding the process.

GitHub Copilot: editor, PR flow, review, CI pipeline. Switching means changing how the team writes software. NetSuite: deep moat (runs the business). Zendesk: light moat (handles tickets). How deep is yours?

No data yet? (Cold-start sidebar)

Synthetic seeding · Transfer learning · Partnership data · HITL bootstrap. The cold-start isn't fatal — it's temporary. Is your architecture designed to escape it?

Case Study · 7 Minutes

The $1.5B Wrapper That Got Eaten Alive

The Bet
Jasper raised $125M at a $1.5B valuation. AI-powered content creation. 70,000+ customers. Fastest-growing AI startup in 2022. The thesis: own the AI writing workflow.
The Crack
ChatGPT launched November 2022. Then OpenAI added browsing, plugins, custom GPTs. Google shipped Gemini into Docs, Sheets, Gmail. AI writing became free, native, everywhere.
The Correction
Jasper "went from 1 to 100 and 100 to 40 within very quick time frames" (Gokul Rajaram). Pivoted to enterprise marketing — brand voice, compliance. The lesson: features get eaten. Workflows survive. Bolt-on AI doesn't change your defensibility score.

Strategic question for the room:

Where is your product a wrapper — and where is it a workflow?

Framework · 3 Minutes

The Kill Switch

A portability architecture that lets you swap AI providers in under 48 hours.
1
Abstraction Layer
A unified API between your product and your AI provider(s). No direct calls to OpenAI/Anthropic/Google in product code. Everything goes through your abstraction.
2
Multi-Model Routing
Different tasks route to different models based on cost, latency, and quality. If one provider fails or reprices, traffic shifts automatically.
3
Evaluation Harness
Automated tests that validate a new provider meets your quality bar before you switch. No manual QA. Switch with confidence.

Switching costs are heading to zero — agents enable data portability, UX can be cloned pixel by pixel. The 48-hour swap test isn't paranoia. It's a survival skill.

If you can't swap providers in 48 hours, you don't have a strategy — you have a dependency.

Framework

Data Flywheel Scoring

Four loops. Score each 1–5. Your weakest loop is where competitors attack.

LoopWhat It MeasuresScore 1Score 5__/5
Correction Do users fix AI outputs? Is that signal captured and reused? No capture Automated retraining —
Preference Does the product learn individual/team preferences over time? Stateless Deep personalization —
Domain Context Does usage in one area improve quality in adjacent areas? Siloed Cross-domain transfer —
Network Does each new user/team make the product better for everyone? Isolated Strong network effects —

Individual Exercise

Map Your Data Flywheel

Your Goal
Your goal is to score your product's four data loops 1–5. For each, write what data you actually capture today and how it compounds.
Keep in Mind
Keep in mind: Your weakest loop is where competitors attack first. That's your investment priority.
Lab Exercise
Steps to follow:
1
Correction loop: When your AI gets something wrong, what happens to that correction? Captured and reused → high. Discarded → low.
2
Preference loop: Does the product learn individual/team preferences? Having analytics ≠ personalization.
3
Domain context loop: Does usage in one area improve quality in adjacent areas? Siloed = 1, cross-domain transfer = 5.
4
Network loop: Does each new user/team make the product better for everyone? Isolated = 1, strong network effects = 5.

Key Takeaways

The Moat

Data in a database isn't a moat — loops are. Most AI products scale without compounding, making them commodities. If your product doesn't get smarter with use, someone else's will.

Self-assessment is always generous. A partner's 90-day encroachment plan surfaces blind spots you can't see yourself. Week-by-week specificity forces honesty about vulnerability.

Vendor portability is strategy, not ops. Can't swap providers in 48 hours? That's not a technology choice — it's a dependency. The Kill Switch turns lock-in into optionality.

Provocation · 8 Minutes

True or False?

Vote first. Then we reveal. Then we argue.
FALSE
"The best AI product wins."
Copycats are everywhere. GPT wrappers with $1B valuations are becoming margin-negative. Even a 10x better product is irrelevant if the model provider ships your feature natively. A remarkable product is necessary — but without moats, it's not sufficient.
FALSE
"You can predict which AI feature will win before launch."
AI systems produce probability distributions, not guarantees. The biggest wins come from non-consumption markets — behaviors that didn't exist before. Pattern matching ("this category is dead") kills these bets.
FALSE
"Your discovery process is fast enough for the AI era."
Traditional discovery takes 6–8 weeks. AI-powered discovery delivers equivalent signal in days. If you're still running quarterly research cycles, your competitors are running daily ones.

By the end of this course, you'll have replaced each of these assumptions with a tested strategy.

Lecture · Point 1 of 3

Annual Planning Cycles Are Obsolete

AI capability curves don't respect fiscal years. The right operating model is continuous weekly/monthly AI-enabled sprints. Leaders still running Q3 roadmap reviews are already behind.

"In pre-AI environments, decisions, features, and outcomes were largely predictable. With AI, outcomes are influenced by data variability, algorithm behavior, and probabilistic models."

— Jeetu Patel, EVP & CPO at Cisco, on The Product Podcast

The shift: from committing to outcomes → making bets under irreducible uncertainty.

Lecture · Point 2 of 3

Discovery Is Too Slow

Traditional discovery — research, synthesis, prioritization — takes 6–8 weeks. AI-powered discovery delivers equivalent signal in days.

Case Evidence
Notion
Used AI-assisted insight clustering to collapse a 6-week research cycle into 4 days during their AI editor rollout.
The Toolkit
AI-Powered Discovery
Synthetic personas. Automated insight synthesis. Real-time segment simulation. 10× velocity without sacrificing signal quality.

The question isn't whether to use AI in discovery. It's whether your competitors already are.

Lecture · Point 3 of 3

Deterministic Thinking in a Probabilistic World

Senior leaders are trained to commit to outcomes. AI systems produce probability distributions, not guarantees. The strategic skill is now: how to make bets under irreducible uncertainty.

THE OLD WAY
"We will ship X feature by Q3 and it will drive Y outcome."
THE NEW WAY
"We have 70% confidence this bet delivers Y. Here's the validation plan and kill criteria."

Google's Bard → Gemini: Not a marketing decision — an acknowledgment that probabilistic product bets require narrative pivots, not apologies. The product didn't fail. The framing did.

The non-consumption insight: The biggest AI bets don't improve existing categories — they create new behaviors. Shopify enabled anyone to sell. Gamma made people buy a presentation tool separately. The TAM looks small because the behavior is new.

Case Study · 7 Minutes

The Company That Validated in 4 Days

The Bet
Notion bet that AI could transform their editor from a writing tool into a thinking tool. Massive surface area, millions of users, high stakes if the AI quality wasn't right.
The Crack
Traditional research would have taken 6–8 weeks of user studies, competitive analysis, and synthesis. By the time they had signal, competitors would have shipped.
The Correction
AI-assisted insight clustering collapsed the research cycle to 4 days. Synthetic persona testing validated use cases before a single line of code. They shipped while competitors were still researching.

The strategic question for the room:

How long does your discovery cycle take today? What decisions are you making slowly that your competitors are making in days?

Framework

The Three-Axis Diagnostic

Three questions that reveal where your AI product strategy breaks.
1
Contextual Moat
How deep is your product embedded in the user's workflow? Could they switch in a weekend?
2
Data Advantage
Do you have proprietary data that compounds over time? Or are you training on the same data as everyone else?
3
Platform Exposure
What happens when OpenAI, Google, or Apple ships your feature natively? How fast can you pivot?

Score each axis 1–5. A 5 means "bulletproof." A 1 means "existential risk." Be honest — your partner's job is to challenge your scores.

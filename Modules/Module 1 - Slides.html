Provocation · 8 Minutes

True or False?

Vote first → reveal → debate.
FALSE
"The best AI product wins."
OpenAI, Google, Apple ship native features — great UX ≠ a moat. Ex: calendar AI vs standalone “AI scheduling” apps.
FALSE
"You can predict which AI feature wins pre-launch."
Outputs are distributions. Big wins often = new behaviors (Shopify, Gamma) — pattern-matching misses them.
FALSE
"Your discovery is fast enough for the AI era."
Classic cycles: 6–8 weeks. AI-assisted loops: days (Notion-class teams). Quarterly research = asymmetric risk.

Key Takeaways

The Bet

AI strategy is probabilistic. Traditional product strategy assumes deterministic outcomes, fixed costs, and annual planning. AI breaks all of these — bets with kill criteria replace promises.

Score your vulnerability honestly. The three-axis diagnostic forces specificity. If all your scores are 5s, you haven't looked hard enough. The partner stress-test and killer memo surface what self-assessment always misses.

Build to think, commit to learn. A working prototype in 15 minutes beats a strategy deck in 15 days. Pushing it to a repo makes it real — version-controlled, shareable, alive. That's what separates builders from deck-builders.

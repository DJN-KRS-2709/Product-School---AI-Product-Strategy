## Slide 5 — True or False
**True or False?** | Provocation · 8 Minutes

Vote first. Then we reveal. Then we argue.

1. **"The best AI product wins."** — FALSE. Copycats are everywhere. GPT wrappers with $1B valuations are becoming margin-negative. The best product is irrelevant if the model provider ships your feature natively.

2. **"You can predict which AI feature will win before launch."** — FALSE. AI systems produce probability distributions, not guarantees. The skill is making bets under irreducible uncertainty.

3. **"Your discovery process is fast enough for the AI era."** — FALSE. Traditional discovery: 6–8 weeks. AI-powered discovery: days. If you're running quarterly research cycles, your competitors are running daily ones.

## Slide 10 — Case Study: Notion
**The Company That Validated in 4 Days** | Case Study · 7 Minutes

Three-act structure (Bet → Crack → Correction):
- **The Bet:** Notion bet AI could transform their editor into a thinking tool. Massive surface area, millions of users, high stakes.
- **The Crack:** Traditional research would have taken 6–8 weeks. Competitors would ship first.
- **The Correction:** AI-assisted insight clustering → 4-day cycle. Shipped while competitors researched.

Strategic question for the room: How long does your discovery cycle take today? What decisions are you making slowly?

## Slide 13 — Three-Axis Diagnostic
**The Three-Axis Diagnostic**

Three cards:
1. **Contextual Moat** — How deep is your product embedded in the workflow? Could they switch in a weekend?
2. **Data Advantage** — Do you have proprietary data that compounds? Or training on the same data as everyone?
3. **Platform Exposure** — What happens when OpenAI/Google/Apple ships your feature natively?

Score each axis 1–5. A 5 means bulletproof. A 1 means existential risk.

- **Slide 11 (Archetypes):** Clean table with color-coded archetype names
- **Slide 13 (Diagnostic):** Three large cards side by side, each with a different color accent
- **Slide 17 (Killer Memo):** Dark/dramatic styling — this is an intense moment
- **Slide 20 (Bridge):** Show the arc flow again with M1 completed (dimmed) and M2 highlighted (next)
